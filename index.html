About me

I come from a conservation background as my father pursued conservation of endangered species while working for the US State Department. Along with his posts in Ireland, Vietnam, and Okinawa, he worked alongside the US Fish and Wildlife Service and started the non-profit Conservation Treaty Support Fund to support CITES, the Convention of the International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora. World Wildlife Fund, and TRAFFIC also contribute to the Convention protecting species like elephants, rhinos and snow leopards, all vulnerable species because of poaching and illegal trade.

Education

With this background, I started out studying Biology at Colorado College. I then pursued Population Biology for my Masters. I studied the Psittacine Species of the South American continent and my Masters was to develop a plan for their conservation. I have a second Masters in Geographical Information Systems at the University of Denver. I was graciously able to work in a partnership between DU and the National Park Service. My continuing education consists of taking GIS courses at CU, here is one of my I now enjoy taking the DU coding bootcamp for web development.


Professional work and goals
